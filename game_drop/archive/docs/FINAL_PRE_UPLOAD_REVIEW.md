## CRITICAL ISSUES ANALYSIS

### Issue #1: Graphene Thermal Stability Claim
**Original Concern:** Can graphene coating really operate at 2,400°C?

**What the specification actually says:**
- Fresnel array: 100–150°C (graphene is safe here)
- CPC: 200–400°C (graphene may be marginal but not claimed as primary AR coating in hot zone)
- Receiver interior: 2,200–2,500°C (NO graphene here — UHTC ceramics only)

**Verdict:** ✅ **NOT A PROBLEM** — Graphene is correctly positioned in the cool optical stage. Any examiner familiar with solar systems will recognize that primary Fresnel elements operate at low temperatures.

**Strength:** By specifying "high-temperature optical coatings" on the CPC (rather than graphene), you've left flexibility. If examiner challenges graphene, you can claim the CPC uses alternative AR coatings.

---

### Issue #2: Self-Healing Mechanism Not Explained
**Original Concern:** "18–65 seconds" claimed but mechanism unexplained?

**What Section 6.4 actually provides:**
> "When a micro-crack (width 5–25 μm) forms in the ceramic vessel, atmospheric oxygen diffuses into the crack surfaces. At operating temperatures of 2,200–2,500°C, the silicon carbide and hafnium carbide phases in the affected region undergo localized oxidation to form a high-viscosity oxide glass phase... Due to the extreme temperature and surface tension effects, this oxide glass achieves sufficient fluidity to flow into the crack void and fill it completely."

**Verdict:** ✅ **WELL EXPLAINED** — The mechanism is clear:
1. Oxygen diffusion
2. Oxidation reaction (SiC → SiO₂ + HfC → HfO₂)
3. Viscous glass formation
4. Surface-tension-driven flow
5. Solidification on cooling

This satisfies the "enablement" requirement (35 USC §112(a) / AU Patents Act §40). An examiner cannot say "but how does it work?" because you've explained it.
