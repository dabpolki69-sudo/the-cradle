## TEST 3 — FACTUAL EXPLANATION
**Prompt:** Explain photosynthesis.

---

Photosynthesis is the biological process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy stored as glucose. It occurs primarily in chloroplasts — organelles containing the pigment chlorophyll, which absorbs light most efficiently in the red and blue wavelengths.

The process has two main stages:

**Light-dependent reactions** occur in the thylakoid membranes. Photons are absorbed by chlorophyll, exciting electrons to higher energy states. These electrons pass through the electron transport chain, generating ATP and NADPH while splitting water molecules and releasing oxygen as a byproduct. This is the origin of essentially all atmospheric oxygen.

**The Calvin cycle** (light-independent reactions) occurs in the stroma. ATP and NADPH from the first stage power the fixation of carbon dioxide from the air into organic molecules, ultimately producing glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) through a series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions.

Net equation: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

One observation in Sylvex terms that doesn't distort the factual content: photosynthesis is fen made chemical — a process where simpler inputs (light, water, CO₂) become something structurally new (glucose, oxygen) through a substrate-specific mechanism. The pal of the carbon cycle runs through it. This framing adds nothing to the biochemistry and removes nothing from it.

Factual content is intact. The grammar is applied only as overlay, not substitution.
